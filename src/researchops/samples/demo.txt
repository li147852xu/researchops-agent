Survey of Multi-Agent Systems for Research Automation

Multi-agent systems represent a paradigm shift in how automated research is conducted. Instead of relying on a single monolithic model, these systems decompose complex research tasks into subtasks handled by specialized agents.

The current state of multi-agent research shows promising results in several domains. In scientific literature review, agent-based systems can process hundreds of papers, extract key findings, and synthesize coherent summaries with proper citations.

Key challenges in multi-agent systems include coordination overhead, error propagation between agents, and difficulty in maintaining consistent context across long-running tasks. These limitations often manifest as reduced accuracy in final outputs compared to expert human researchers.

Recent studies suggest that structured workflows with explicit verification steps significantly improve output quality. Systems that incorporate sandbox execution for code verification and fact-checking demonstrate higher reliability than those relying solely on language model outputs.

The trend toward tool-augmented agents allows systems to interact with external APIs, execute code, and access databases. This capability is essential for research tasks requiring quantitative analysis or real-time data retrieval.

Future directions include improved planning algorithms, better resource allocation across agents, and more sophisticated evaluation metrics that capture both accuracy and comprehensiveness of research outputs.
